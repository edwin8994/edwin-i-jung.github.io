About Me

I am a candidate with Master's degree in Materials Science and Engineering at SNML, KAIST, supervised by Prof. Sang Ouk Kim, and following working experience of 3 years and 1 month as a research officer-in-charge at Ewha Womans University Cooperation Foundation. Before pursuing my master's degree, I received a bachelor's degree in Materials Science and Engineering from Korea University in 2020.

My research focuses on block copolymer self-assembly and its application into various fields such as sensors, batteries, and anti-counterfeiting labels. Through all these projects, I performed procedures such as photolithography, plasma etching, and metal deposition. I also have an experience of realizing directed self-assembly of block copolymers using graphoepitaxy method through photolithography with my can-do attitude.

I hold dual nationalities for the USA and South Korea, and I have completed my mandatory military service for South Korea.
